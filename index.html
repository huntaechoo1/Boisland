GLORY TO BOISLAND

Boisland

Boisland is a nation in Europe, consisting of a large island off the west of the Iberian Peninsula and Britain.

Boisland was inhabited as early as the Lower Paleolithic period. The name Boisland was designated as a formal name for the nation by its four founding fathers, Jerry, Pedro, Sunchel, and Hunter. It is a traditional maritime power, with a wealth of ocean resources and one of the world's largest trading fleets. Located in a strategic trade route and with an abundance of natural resources, Boisland is one of the world's leading economic leaders.

Social atmosphere in Boisland is reported to promote patriotism, pragmaticism, and productivity. Boisland also has a well structured higher educated system and has produced many of the world's leading thinkers. Academics have also attributed the success of Boisland to its unusual political system, which lacks a parliament or congress in favor of a Board of Guilds, which serves as its legislative as well as executive branch.
